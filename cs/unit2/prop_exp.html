Exponents

Early in the development of mathematical notation, it was recognized that writing: $x\cdot x\cdot x\cdot x\cdot x$ was perhaps not the most efficient way of communicating the idea that $x$ is multiplied by itself five times. Instead, we introduce the exponent notation and write $x^5$. We will see that many of the familiar properties of exponents arise just from this idea.

Properties of Exponents:
  • $x^a\cdot x^b= x^{a+b}$
  • $(x^a)^b=x^{ab}$
  • $x^0=1$ for all nonzero $x$
  • $\dfrac{1}{x^a}=x^{-a}$

$4^{-3}$ and $\dfrac{1}{4^3}$ represent the same number. Why would you prefer one to the other?

By our properties of exponents we can see that $(x^{1/2})^2=x$ holds for any $x$. This will help us make sense of what we mean by fractional exponents. Let's start with something like $3^{1/2}$. We know that when we square it we will get 3, but we already have a symbol for the number that squares to 3: $\sqrt{3}$. Thus, $3^{1/2}=\sqrt{3}$. Applying this to other fractions we see that fractional exponents allow us to express, $\sqrt[a]{x^b}$ more simply as $x^{b/a}$.
