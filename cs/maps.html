Traditional Maps of the City

The two pictures above are of traditional geographic maps of the bronx and new york city. Both maps serve the general purpose of showing the geographic relation among various physical locations, as well as the political boundaries established at the time the maps were made. Notice the map on the left has additional information regarding the physical nature of the shoreline.

The following are links to a few maps of the city to illustrate different ways of mapping the same physical space, but adding some additional information tied to the traditional physical space.

For each of the above maps think about what additional information is displayed. What do you think is the creator's intention? What conclusions can you draw? What conclusions might you draw at first that later do not bear out?
