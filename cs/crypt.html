Codes

First some terminology. Plain text is the message you want to send and be understood. This is then encrypted in some way to generate cipher text. The method of generating the ciper text is called the cipher. In general there are two main methods of encrypting a message.

  • Transposition or rearrangement of all the letters of the message.
  • Substitution of each letter for a different letter.

Transposition

Generally these messages are difficult to decode and encode if the message has a lot of characters. One of the earliest recorded methods was the Spartan scytale. You can implement a scytale system by using some tape, paper and a pencil to wind the paper around.

One type of common transposition method is called the rail-fence transposition. It involves taking your message and breaking it into two rows; the odd numbered characters in row one and the even numbered characters in row two. Then you generate the cipher text by writing row 1 followed by row 2 in a single line of text.

Using the rail-fence transposition generate the cipher text of the plain text "secret messages are secret".
scemsaeaeertertesgsrsce
With a partner use the rail-fence transposition generate the cipher text from a plain text that you make up. Have your partner decode the cipher text.

Substitution

The simplest substitution method is often called the Caesar shift cipher. This involves substituting letters in the plaintext with letters n further in the alphabet. Hence, if the particular cipher were a shift of 3 letters then $A\to D$ and $L\to O$ and so on.

Use the Caesar shift cipher of 5 letters to encrypt the plain text "secret messages are secret".
xjhwjyrjxxfljxfwjxjhwjy

One of the first substitution ciphers that is based upon a key word works as follows:

  1. Choose a keyword--in this example "lucky"

  2. Write out your keyword and then follow it with the alphabet in its normal ordering without the letters in your keyword.

    In our example our alphabet would be written luckyabdefghikmnopqrstvwxz

  3. Then the substitution works by associating each plain text letter with the letter below it in the table below.
    abcdefghijklmnopqrstuvwxyz
    luckyabdefghikmnopqrstvwxz
Use the substitution cipher based on the key word "lucky" to encrypt the plain text "secret messages are secret".
qycpyriyqqlbyqlpyqycpyr

Another example of a substitution cipher is called the pigpen cipher. This one substitues symbols for letters of plaintext.

Use the pigpen cipher to encrypt the plain text "secret messages are secret".
